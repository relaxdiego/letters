title: If this site disappears
---

If you're reading this page, maybe nothing is wrong yet and you're only
curious. Good. Read it anyway, so that you know where things are.

Websites don't last. Domain names have to be paid for. Companies close. Someday
`letters.maglana.com` will stop working, and I won't be around to fix it. So
I'm writing down every place these letters are kept and how to get them back.
You don't need to be a programmer and if any of this feels difficult, hand this
page to an AI assistant and ask it to walk you through the step you are stuck
on.

The letters exist in three places beyond this website. That might seem overkill
but I would rather over-prepare than have you go looking for me one day and find
nothing there.

## 1. The Wayback Machine

The Wayback Machine is a library that keeps copies of old web pages. Every
time I published a letter, a copy was sent there automatically. Go to:

<https://web.archive.org/web/*/letters.maglana.com>

You will see a calendar. Days with a coloured circle are days a copy was
saved. Click any circle, then click a time, and you will see the site as it
looked on that day.

## 2. The repository itself

The raw files live at:

<https://github.com/relaxdiego/letters>

This website is a facade put in front of the letters to make them more visually
presentable. **The letters themselves are the plain `.md` files inside the
`content/letters/` folder.** Click any one of them. You will see the letter,
with a title and a date at the top. No special program is needed to read them,
you can use any text editor. You can also download the whole thing at once with
the green **Code** button, then **Download ZIP**, and keep it somewhere safe.

If you want the website back, or turn it into a book, hand the whole repository
to an AI assistant and ask it something like:

> This repository contains letters from my father. Please turn them into a
> print-ready PDF book, ordered by date.

Or:

> Please rebuild the website from this repository so I can read it on my own
> computer.

The file `AGENTS.md` in the repository tells that assistant everything it
needs to know. It's written for exactly this moment.

## 3. Paper

This step is the one I can't promise you.

As I write this, no printed copy of these letters exists. I hope to make one.
Perhaps by the time you read this I will have, and there is a book on a shelf
somewhere in the family, so ask your mother, your brother, your cousins, your
aunts, and uncles.

But maybe I never got around to it. If so, I'm sorry, you'll have to do the
thing I did not. Print these letters. Have them bound. Keep one, and give one
to someone who does not live in your house. Paper needs no electricity, no
password, and no company that still exists. It is the only link in this chain
that does not depend on the world staying the way it is.

---

That is the whole chain. Two of those three places are real today, they are run
by different people in different countries, and losing the letters would take
both failing at once. The third is up to whoever reads this. It may be me. It
may be you.

Take care of yourselves. Take care of each other.

— Dad
